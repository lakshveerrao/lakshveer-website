# Lakshveer Rao
*Person — Central Subject*

## Identity

**Lakshveer Rao** (born 2017, age 8-9) is an Indian hardware founder and maker from Hyderabad, India. He is the co-founder of **Projects by Laksh** and creator of **CircuitHeroes** — an educational electronics card game. Recognized as India's youngest hardware startup founder.

Currently in Grade 2. Operating at the intersection of hardware electronics, robotics, AI agents, and computer vision. Building and shipping real products while documenting 170+ project builds publicly on YouTube.

Father: CaptVenk ([@CaptVenk](https://x.com/CaptVenk)) — frequent collaborator and co-builder.

## What He Builds

**Shipped Products:**
- **CircuitHeroes** — Educational electronics card game teaching component knowledge. 300+ decks sold. Available on Amazon. [[sig-circuitheroes-launch-2024]], [[sig-malpani-grant-2025]]
- **The Kids Book of Creative Ideas** — DIY eBook. 100+ sales via ChhotaCreator.com. [[sig-ebook-launch-2024]]
- **Drishtikon Yantra** — Raspberry Pi-based wearable assistive vision device for visually impaired users. Special Mention at Param × Vedanta Makeathon. [[sig-drishtikon-2026]], [[sig-param-award-2025]]
- **MotionX** — Full-body motion-control gaming system. Special Mention Winner at RunTogether Hackathon (Runable). [[sig-motionx-launch-2026]]
- **Hardvare** — Hardware intelligence platform combining physical devices with AI agents. [[sig-hardvare-system-2026]]
- **OpenClaw / ZeroClaw** — Autonomous AI agent system for grant discovery and application. [[sig-openclaw-agent-2026]], [[sig-autoclaw-system-2026]]
- **Kyabol** — AI conversational system demoed at Gemini 3 Hackathon (Cerebral Valley × Google DeepMind). [[sig-gemini3-hackathon-2026]]
- **AI Agent → ESP32 Hardware Control** — Telegram messaging interface controlling physical hardware. National media coverage (Financial Express, StartupPedia). [[sig-fe-mait-agent-2026]], [[sig-startuppedia-2026]]

**Content Production:**
- YouTube channel [@ProjectsByLaksh](https://youtube.com/@ProjectsByLaksh) — 170+ documented builds across electronics, robotics, Arduino, Raspberry Pi, AI. [[sig-yt-170-projects-2025]]
