# education
*Domain*

## Summary
Lakshveer Rao operates at the intersection of peer education and hardware learning through product creation. His educational work spans two modalities: creating learning tools (CircuitHeroes card game, DIY eBook) and embodying learner-as-maker publicly (Grade 2 student featured for curiosity). Educational products achieved commercial validation (300+ CircuitHeroes decks, 100+ eBook sales) and institutional recognition (₹1,00,000 Malpani Foundation grant).

## Evidence
**Products as pedagogy:** CircuitHeroes card game launched October 2024 as educational electronics tool [[sig-circuitheroes-launch-2024]]. Described as work of "India's youngest card game designer." DIY eBook "The Kids Book of Creative Ideas" launched September 2024, sold 100+ copies via ChhotaCreator.com [[sig-ebook-launch-2024]].

**Institutional validation:** Malpani Foundation awarded ₹1,00,000 grant June 2025 explicitly for CircuitHeroes' educational innovation [[sig-malpani-grant-2025]]. ThinkTac featured Lakshveer June 2025 as "Grade 2 student who loves exploring how things work" [[sig-think-tac-feature-2025]].

## Trajectory
**Phase 1 (2024-09 to 2024-10):** Product launches. eBook precedes card game by 1 month.

**Phase 2 (2025-06):** Convergence event. Grant award, media feature, and four endorsements occur same month. Suggests coordinated credentialing or natural clustering around milestone.

**Commercial traction:** 300+ CircuitHeroes decks represents ~₹3,00,000+ revenue at ₹999 typical pricing. 100+ eBook sales at digital price point. Scale modest but validates product-market fit for peer-created educational content.

**Pedagogical model:** Learn-by-making → teach-by-selling → scale-through-product. Lakshveer's education strategy is commercial distribution of learning artifacts, not curriculum or classroom presence.
