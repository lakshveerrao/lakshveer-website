# hardware
*Domain*

## Summary
Hardware represents Lakshveer's foundation as a maker, demonstrated through multiple physical computing projects from age 8 onwards. His work spans from circuit-based educational tools to ESP32-controlled AI agents and assistive technology devices. This domain showcases his ability to bridge software intelligence with real-world actuators and sensors.

## Evidence
Lakshveer's hardware capabilities are documented across multiple projects and recognitions:

**CircuitHeroes** — An educational hardware project that earned the Malpani Foundation Grant in June 2025 [[sig-malpani-grant-2025]] and was presented at South Park Commons Demo Day in August 2025 [[sig-spc-demo-2025]].

**Drishtikon Yantra** — An assistive vision device for the visually impaired, demonstrated as hardware innovation alongside CircuitHeroes at SPC [[sig-spc-demo-2025]] and later documented in January 2026 [[sig-drishtikon-2026]].

**ESP32 AI Agent** — Beginning in March 2026, Lakshveer built an AI agent system running on ESP32 hardware that could be controlled via Telegram messaging [[sig-startuppedia-2026]]. This project was characterized by RediMinds as "an ESP32 agent that codes" [[sig-rediminds-linkedin-2026]] and by Somi AI as an "AI agent that controls real hardware" [[sig-somi-ai-linkedin-2026]]. The build received extensive documentation through YouTube content [[sig-yt-short-telegram-2026]], [[sig-yt-short-2-2026]], [[sig-yt-full-2026]].

**National Recognition** — His hardware work contributed to receiving the PARAM Award for young innovation in October 2025 [[sig-param-award-2025]] and media coverage in Beats in Brief in September 2025 [[sig-beats-in-brief-2025]].

## Trajectory
Lakshveer's hardware journey shows progression from educational circuit tools (CircuitHeroes, 2025) to assistive technology (Drishtikon Yantra, 2025-2026) to AI-integrated embedded systems (ESP32 agent, 2026). The timeline reveals increasing complexity: from static educational hardware to real-time AI-controlled actuators. By age 8 (March 2026), he had achieved sufficient technical depth to build systems where language models controlled physical hardware through microcontroller platforms.

The shift from pure hardware projects to AI-hardware integration represents a significant evolution in capability, combining embedded systems programming, wireless communication protocols, and agent-based control systems.
