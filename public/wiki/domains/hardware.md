# hardware
*Domain*

## Summary

Lakshveer began building hardware at age 4 with a DC motor fan [[sig-first-dc-motor-2022]] and has since become recognized as India's youngest hardware startup founder. His work spans PCB design, embedded systems (ESP32, Raspberry Pi), robotics, assistive technology, and AI-hardware integration. He has won placement at national hackathons, signed a co-creation agreement with Lion Circuits [[sig-lion-circuits-cocreation-2025]], and launched multiple physical products including CircuitHeroes (300+ decks sold), Drishtikon Yantra (assistive vision wearable), and the Hardvare platform.

## Evidence

**First Build (2022)**  
At age 4, Lakshveer documented his first hardware project: a DC motor fan [[sig-first-dc-motor-2022]]. This marks the earliest verifiable hardware work.

**CircuitHeroes (2024–2025)**  
Educational electronics card game designed to teach circuit concepts. Teaser launched 2024-10 [[sig-circuitheroes-launch-2024]]. Sold 300+ decks. Earned ₹1,00,000 grant from Malpani Foundation 2025-06 [[sig-malpani-grant-2025]]. Demoed at South Park Commons, Bangalore [[sig-spc-demo-2025]].

**Competitions & Recognition (2025)**  
- Youngest participant at Hardware Hackathon 1.0 (Lion Circuits × PCB Cupid) 2025-07 — built hydration assistant using Glyph board + sensor [[sig-lion-circuits-hw1-2025]]  
- Top-5 Finalist (youngest) at Hardware Hackathon 2.0, 2025-07 [[sig-lion-circuits-hw2-2025]]  
- Recognized at IITEX 2025, Hitex Hyderabad [[sig-iitex-2025]]  
- Attended drone workshop at IIT Hyderabad 2025-06 [[sig-iit-hyd-drone-workshop-2025]]

**Lion Circuits Partnership (2025–11)**  
Signed co-creation agreement with Lion Circuits, a PCB manufacturing company [[sig-lion-circuits-cocreation-2025]].

**Assistive Tech: Drishtikon Yantra (2026-01)**  
Raspberry Pi-based wearable assistive vision device for visually impaired users. Special Mention at Param × Vedanta Makeathon [[sig-drishtikon-2026]]. Uses computer vision for real-time scene understanding.

**AI-Hardware Integration (2026-01–03)**  
Built AI agent that controls ESP32 hardware via Telegram messaging [[sig-startuppedia-2026]] [[sig-yt-short-telegram-2026]]. Featured by StartupNews.fyi, RediMinds, Somi AI, Dileep Gupta [[sig-startupnewsfyi-ig-2026]] [[sig-rediminds-linkedin-2026]] [[sig-somi-ai-linkedin-2026]] [[sig-dileep-gupta-linkedin-2026]].

**MotionX (2026-01)**  
Full-body motion-control gaming system built at RunTogether Hackathon by Runable. Special Mention Winner [[sig-motionx-launch-2026]]. Integrated computer vision with physical hardware for gesture recognition.

**Hardvare Platform (2026-01)**  
Hardware intelligence platform combining physical devices with AI agents [[sig-hardvare-system-2026]]. Represents synthesis of embedded systems, AI, and networked hardware control.
