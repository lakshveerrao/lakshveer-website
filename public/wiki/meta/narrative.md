## The Arc

Lakshveer's trajectory follows a clear progression from hardware fundamentals to AI-native systems. His early years (age 4–7) focused on electronics fundamentals with Arduino and Raspberry Pi builds, documented methodically on YouTube. By 2025, he had shifted from pure documentation to product creation — CircuitHeroes represented his first commercial offering, combining electronics education with game mechanics and achieving 300+ deck sales.

The ISRO meeting in April 2025 marked institutional recognition of his hardware capabilities, while the South Park Commons demo in August 2025 positioned him within India's startup ecosystem. His participation in competitive environments (Scaler finals, VibeHack) demonstrated ability to operate under constraints.

The 2026 pivot toward AI agents represents architectural evolution. Drishtikon Yantra demonstrates social impact focus, applying computer vision to accessibility challenges.

Media coverage accelerated in March 2026 following the Telegram agent demo, with features across startup media (StartupPedia, RediMinds, StartupG), developer communities (Reddit r/IndiaTech), and video platforms (YouTube Shorts).
