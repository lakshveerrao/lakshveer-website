# Lakshveer Rao — Narrative
*Meta Article*

## Summary

Lakshveer Rao (born 2018, Hyderabad) is a hardware founder and maker who began building electronics at age 4. By age 8, he had shipped three commercial products: CircuitHeroes (educational electronics card game, 300+ decks sold), The Kids Book of Creative Ideas (eBook, 100+ sales), and Drishtikon Yantra (assistive vision device). Documented 170+ electronics, robotics, and AI projects on YouTube. Selected for The Residency Delta-2 cohort (youngest founder ever), received grants from Malpani Foundation (₹1,00,000) and AI Grants India, and demonstrated work to ISRO Chairman S. Somanath. Active in hardware hackathons, AI agent development, and public speaking at venues including IIT Hyderabad, T-Hub, and August Fest.

## The Arc

**Phase 1: Builder Origins (2022-2023, Age 4-5)**
Lakshveer's public journey began at age 4 with YouTube documentation of basic electronics projects — drill bit repair, DC motor fans. Father Venkatesh (CaptVenk) documented builds on YouTube channel "Projects by Laksh." Foundation in hands-on hardware learning.

**Phase 2: Product Formation (2024, Age 6-7)**
Transition from documentation to productization. First business pitch in March 2024. Launched two educational products: The Kids Book of Creative Ideas (eBook, 100+ sales) and CircuitHeroes (electronics card game, 300+ decks sold by June 2025). Regional media recognition in Telugu press. Labeled "India's youngest card game designer."

**Phase 3: Recognition & Validation (2025, Age 7-8)**
Institutional validation accelerated. Demonstrated projects to ISRO Chairman S. Somanath (April). Taught IoT at IIT Hyderabad. Received Malpani Foundation grant (₹1,00,000) and AI Grants India funding. Selected for The Residency Delta-2 cohort as youngest founder ever. Won/placed at multiple hardware hackathons (Lion Circuits Hardware Hackathon 1.0 youngest participant, Hardware Hackathon 2.0 Top-5, Scaler Innovation Lab top 8 of 200 teams). Spoke at August Fest, T-Hub, South Park Commons. National media coverage (Beats in Brief, Financial Express). Social validation from founders (Roohi Kirit, Ramsri Goutham, Shantanu Goel).

**Phase 4: Advanced Systems (2025-2026, Age 8)**
Shift toward complex AI-hardware integration. Built Drishtikon Yantra (Raspberry Pi assistive vision device for visually impaired, Special Mention at Param × Vedanta Makeathon). MotionX (full-body motion-control gaming, Special Mention at RunTogether Hackathon). AI agent controlling ESP32 hardware via Telegram messaging (viral coverage: Financial Express, StartupPedia, Reddit). Developed Claw AI system (OpenClaw/ZeroClaw autonomous grant agents). Participated in Gemini 3 Hackathon (Cerebral Valley × Google DeepMind). Signed co-creation agreement with Lion Circuits.

**Current State (2026)**
170+ documented projects on YouTube. Three shipped commercial products. Active in hardware hackathons, AI agent development, computer vision applications. Building Hardvare (hardware intelligence platform). Age 8.
