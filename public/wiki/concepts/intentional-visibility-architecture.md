## Significance

This pattern reveals infrastructure behind child prodigy narratives. Technical competency (building functional AI agents, computer vision systems) is *necessary but insufficient* for visibility. The systematic conversion of technical work into multi-platform content suggests:

1. **Production Pipeline:** Standardized workflow for capturing, editing, and distributing project documentation across formats
2. **Media Relationships:** Access to startup/tech press (Financial Express, StartupPedia) uncommon for individual builders
3. **Social Capital:** Organizations (RediMinds, Somi AI) voluntarily amplifying work suggests existing network position
4. **Strategic Framing:** Age consistently emphasized ("8-year-old") in headlines—deliberate positioning in "child prodigy" narrative category

The architecture scales: early projects (2025) show 1-2 signals, recent work (2026) generates 8-12+. This suggests either iterative learning or increasing investment in visibility infrastructure.

**Implications for "Child Prodigy" Phenomenon:**
- Visibility disparity likely reflects parental/mentor resources, not just technical ability
- Many equally skilled young builders may lack amplification infrastructure
- Success metrics shift from "what was built" to "how many saw what was built"
