## Significance

This dual-track pattern reveals strategic sequencing rather than random opportunity capture. Traditional institutions (ISRO, national awards, established foundations) provided credibility tokens in April-October 2025 that facilitated entry into peer networks (SPC, Scaler, startup media) where 16-year-olds typically face skepticism. 

The timing is critical: South Park Commons demo (August 2025) occurs 4 months after ISRO meeting and 2 months after Malpani grant — suggesting institutional validation created leverage for founder-community access. By March 2026, media coverage drops youth-prodigy framing entirely, indicating successful transition from "impressive for his age" to "builder shipping products."

This pattern demonstrates awareness that entrepreneurial communities prioritize evidence of shipping (CircuitHeroes sales numbers, Drishtikon Yantra hardware) over credentials, but that institutional gatekeepers can accelerate the credibility accumulation process for young builders. The gap between institutional validation (October 2025 PARAM Award) and peer-network media (March 2026 StartupG feature) suggests a 5-month integration period.
