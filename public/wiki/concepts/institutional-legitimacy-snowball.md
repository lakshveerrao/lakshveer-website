## Summary

Between February 2025 and March 2026, Lakshveer Rao transitions from novelty ("youngest founder") to institutional peer through a compounding legitimacy cycle. ## Significance

The legitimacy snowball demonstrates how institutional endorsements compound: ISRO Chairman access opens IIT teaching opportunities; hackathon wins enable accelerator applications; accelerator membership grants peer status in builder communities. Each validation layer makes the next more accessible. By 2026, media coverage shifts from "child prodigy" framing to technical merit — Financial Express covers the *what* (AI agent architecture) before the *who* (8-year-old). Runable's acknowledgment that Lakshveer "changed what we thought was possible" indicates he has altered institutional priors, not just satisfied them.

The pattern suggests institutions initially grant access as novelty/PR, but sustained technical output converts novelty into peer status. The Residency Delta-2 selection is inflection point: youngest ever admitted, but admitted on same terms as adult founders.
